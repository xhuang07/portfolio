This identity is inspired by Hong Kong neon culture which is also the original city for Dim Sum.
Nom Wah Tea Parlor is the first Dim Sum Place in Manhattan. Hidden in the corner of Doyer street, Nom Wah hasn’t gone through many changes throughout the years. Its vinatage looks,good food and great value has kept generations of families coming back. This is a rebranding project for Nom Wah Tea Parlor.
Hong Kong was populated with neon signs, the city’s layers are made up of glowing symbols and architectures. But in recent years, the making of neon lights has become a dying craft and visual culture, I wish to bring up people’s recognition to the intangible cultural heritage while they are enjoying this type of cuisine.
